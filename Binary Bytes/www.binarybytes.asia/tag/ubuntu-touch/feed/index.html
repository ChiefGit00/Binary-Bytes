The first phone to us the OS is from the Spanish handset maker BQ called Aquarius E4.5 Ubuntu Edition at a very cheap price.

 SoC MediaTek
 CPU Quad-core @ 1.3 GHz Cortex A7
 CPU Mali 400 @ 500 MHz
RAM 1 GB
Storage 8 GB (5.5 GB available) + microSD slot
OS Ubuntu OS
Display 4.5″ (960 x 540) IPS with Protective Dragontrail
Camera 5 MP (rear) and 2 MP (front)
Battery Capacity 3220 mAh (non-removable Li-Po)
Dimensions 137 x 67 x 9 mm
Weight 123 grams

The highlight of the phones are a 5 megapixel front facing camera

Ubuntu which had the idea to connect smartphone with a dock to enable the desktop mode seems not to be working with this phone at least. A new service called “Scope” which originally debuted as lenses in desktop Ubuntu are the defining feature.

Canonical says Scope is a UI paradigm that consists of categorized home pages : one home page for news, one for music, etc.

Ubuntu Aquarius will have a tough competition in the market mainly from Moto E. Buyers will have to follow  Ubuntu’s Twitter handle and wait for a series Flash Sales over the next few weeks.

Early buyers will have to wait for apps to be developed for the phone while basic apps such as Facebook and Twitter are preserved at least. The apps are developed on HTML 5.

The sale for phone will go in Europe for €170 (₹ 12,000).

Canonical had unveiled the Ubuntu phone platform on 2 January 2013 and had an unsuccessful crowdfunding on Indigogo for a dual booting high end phone (Ubuntu Edge) in the same year. Images of the OS have been available for many Nexus and other phones.
