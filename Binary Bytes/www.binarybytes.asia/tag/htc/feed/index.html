It is all in all the same phone as the original HTC M8 with only the 4 Ultra Pixel back camera replaced by 13 MP sensor. It still retains the dual depth sensing camera from the original, used to collect depth data to add post picture focus manipulation. Plus the best in class BoomSound front stereo speakers.

HTC M8 Eye is a high end phone is priced at Rs 38,990 (US $649) and will only be launched in India and China.

HTC Re

Along with these two HTC also launched the one of its kind camera called Re. It is a small form factor 16 MP camera without a viewfinder so that we many spend our time absorbing the event in real time with our eyes instead of a digital display.

It is said to exclusive to Snapdeal till stock runs out and costs Rs. Rs. 9,990 ($150), though we were yet not able to find any listing.

The phone was announced at an event in Tokyo, Japan today. It is the international version of J Butterfly HTL23 released in Japan.

HTC says that the new Boom Sound is 25% louder than the one in HTC One. They  partnered with HARMAN to include “LiveStage Signal Processing Technology” Digital Signal Processor (DSP). Also the phone will come with exclusively designed JBL in-ear-headphones.

Butterfly 2 is IPX5 and IPX7 certified making it water and dustproof.

Like HTC One, it too has ‘Dual’ cameras. The second camera is used to capture depth data. This allows for pictures to be refocused in software after capturing (like the Lytro camera).

It will go on sale across Asia from next month.
Also known as HTC J Butterfly it will be available in Taiwan on September 2 and later in Hong Kong, India, Malaysia, Singapore and Thailand. No word on price yet.
