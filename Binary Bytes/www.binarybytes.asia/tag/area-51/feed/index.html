The gaming beast is based on the soon to be released Intel Haswell-E processors along with the X99 platform. Haswell-E are the six and eight core desktop variants of last year’s Haswell microarcitecture. The X99 platform is the debut of DDR4 memories too.
This new 2014 Area-51 design is called by the makers ‘Triad’. The unique triangular design seen first time in a desktop system give it some unique properties. The airflow is not obstructed even when it is kept close to a wall. It also allows for much easier access to the rear IO ports. It has a handle at the top and the chassis can be pulled a little to the front.

As usual it does not skimp on Alienware’s array of software controlled lightning system around the chassis called AlienFX

The full ATX chassis of the  new Area 51 supports up to three full-width GPUs ( Nvidia or AMD ), up to five hard drives or SSDs, and is designed to support a 1.5K watt power supply.

It also has a built-in Killer Gigabit gaming NIC with 802.11ac WiFi.

The new Area 51 will start shipping in October in the US, and later in the Holiday season globally.
The starting price has not been announced, but few doubts it will cost a lot.

It can optionally be equipped with a Dell UltraSharp 34″ monitor. It is an ultra-wide 21:9 screen with WQHD (3440 x 1440) resolution and operates at 60 hertz.
The monitor (U3415W) will be available to order in China in November and globally in December of this year.
