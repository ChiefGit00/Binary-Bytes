 SoC NVIDIA Tegra K1
 CPU Dual-core 2.3 GHz Denver 64-bit
 CPU Kepler DX1 (192 CUDA Cores)
RAM 2GB
Storage 16 GB, 32 GB and 32 GB + LTE (non expandable)
OS  Android 5.0 (Lollipop)
Display 8.9″ (2047 x 1535, 4:3)  IPS LCD

NEXUS 9 comes with a magnetic docking keyboard that double acts as a case. Seems to be inspired by Microsoft’s Surface Touch/Type Cover.

It is not only the first Android 5.0 (Lollipop) (read about the OS from the June event here) tablet but also the first NEXUS device to run on 64-bit architecture. The SoC is nVidia’s latest from the Tegra line. NEXUS 9 however is the first to come with the nVidia designed Denver CPU cores instead of the licensed ARM cores used previously.

The display aspect ratio (4:3) choice is interesting. Andorid tablets are usually 16:9 or 16:10 widescreen displays, iPads have been 4:3 all along. Adoring the front are the HTC’s trademark BoomSound dual front-facing speakers.

It can be pre-ordered starting 17 October and will become available in stores (Google Play and also Amazon, Best Buy, Radio Shack, Staples or Walmart) from 3rd November in the USA first.

Prices :

16 GB = $399 (Rs. 24,500)
32 GB = $479 (Rs. 30,000)
32 GB with LTE = $599 (~Rs. 37,000)
