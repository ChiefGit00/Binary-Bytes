Intel’s GM of of it’s Middle East and North African region, Taha Khalifa said that they will soon be releasing their 10nm CPUs in the market.

He said “We have been consistently pursuing Moore’s Law and this has been the core of our innovation for the last 40 years. The 10nm chips are expected to be launched early 2017″.

Update (2016)

Intel has discarded it’s tock tick tock (new µacrch -> die shrink -> new µarch) guidelines and adopted a more conservative approach (new µarch -> µarch optimization -> die shrink).

Thus now the current road map of Intel is

2014 = 14 nm (Broadwell)
2015 = new µarch (Skylake)
2016 = µarch optimization (Kabylake)
2017 = 10nm (?)
